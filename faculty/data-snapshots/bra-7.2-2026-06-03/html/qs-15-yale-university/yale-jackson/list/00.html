🥂 Celebrating the 2025 Yale World Fellows
🌍 This year, the Yale World Fellows Program brought together 16 extraordinary leaders working across environment, technology, politics, media, the arts, human rights, international development, and justice — from a Nigerian singer-songwriter to a Turkish politician to a Pakistani police superintendent.

Throughout their time at Yale, the Fellows inspired the @yale, #YaleJackson, and #NewHaven communities, mentored students, and supported one another with generosity and purpose. On December 9, we were proud to mark their achievements at a closing ceremony celebrating their leadership and impact.

“We must not succumb to cynicism,” said Emma Sky, Director of the Yale International Leadership Center. “What the world needs now is not more despair, but more vision… leaders who can stretch our imagination of what is possible — and inspire good people to act.”

Remarks were offered by Sky, Aleena Gul ’26, the World Fellows Program’s longtime Student Ambassador, and Fellow Wei Xing.

Once a Fellow, always a Fellow. 💙

🌍 Yale students had the rare opportunity to meet two former heads of state and attend the launch of our #YaleJackson`s new Democracy Dialogues series.

With generous support from @libraphilanthropies, the event brought together:
🇨🇴 Iván Duque Márquez, former President of Colombia
🇬🇷 George Papandreou, former Prime Minister of Greece
🌐 George Logothetis, Executive Chairman of Libra Group

Moderated by Emma Sky, Director of Yale International Leadership Center, the conversation explored crisis leadership, democratic resilience, and the innovations needed for renewal in the 21st century.

These dialogues are part of what makes Yale Jackson a close-knit, globally engaged community — where students don’t just study leadership, they meet the leaders shaping our world. ✨
